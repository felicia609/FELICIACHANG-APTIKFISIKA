Dynamic Electricity

Electric Potential & Potential Difference

Electric potential is the energy per unit charge needed to move a charge in an electric field. It is measured in volts (V), with 1 volt equating to 1 joule per coulomb.

Electric Current

Electric current is the rate at which charge flows per second, measured in amperes (A). A current of 1 A means 1 coulomb of charge flows per second.

Ohm’s Law

Ohm’s Law states that voltage (V) equals current (I) times resistance (R):

V = I × R

Kirchhoff’s Laws

Kirchhoff’s Current Law (KCL) states that the total current entering a junction equals the total current leaving. Kirchhoff’s Voltage Law (KVL) states that the sum of voltages in a closed loop is zero.

Series & Parallel Circuits

In series circuits, current is the same through all components, while in parallel circuits, voltage remains constant across branches.

Electromotive Force (EMF)

EMF is the energy provided per charge by a source like a battery, even when no current flows.

Electric Sources

Electric sources include batteries, generators, and solar cells, each converting different forms of energy into electrical energy.

Resistors

Resistors control current flow, following Ohm’s Law. They are used for current limiting, voltage division, and signal conditioning.
