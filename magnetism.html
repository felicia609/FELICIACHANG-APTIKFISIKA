Magnetism

Magnetic Poles

Every magnet has a North and South Pole where the magnetic field is strongest. Opposite poles attract, while like poles repel. If a magnet is cut in half, each piece will still have both poles.

Attract and Repel

If both magnet are the same pole it will repel but if the both magnets are opposite poles (North and South) it will attract.

Materials That Attract Magnet

Magnets are materials that produce a magnetic field, which can attract or repel other materials. Common magnetic materials include iron, nickel, cobalt, and certain alloys.

Methods of Magnetization and Demagnetization

Magnetization:

  • Contact Method
  • Electrical Method
  • Hammering in a Magnetic Field

Demagnetization:

  • Heating above Curie temperature
  • Hammering
  • Applying an Alternating Current

Magnetic Field and Right-Hand Rule

A current-carrying wire produces a circular magnetic field. The right-hand rule helps determine its direction: thumb points in current direction, and fingers curl in the direction of the field.

Electromagnetism and Applications

Electromagnets are used in motors, transformers, MRI machines, and lifting magnets.
